Haarlem

Get to the heart of things at the Grote Markt

At the historic heart of Haarlem, this vast square is located underneath the imposing Grote Kerk, and is the image of the city that many people will recognise. Hosting regular festivals, concerts and markets, the Grote Markt is the hub of daily life in Haarlem and just happens to be one of the prettiest city squares in the country. Go there on a Saturday when the Netherlands’ finest street market rolls out its wares.

View Haarlem from the water

Amsterdam isn’t the only city famous for its canal cruises. Haarlem also has a close connection to water, with the Spaarne river meandering through the heart of city and various canals and waterways weaving their way between ancient monuments and sights. It’s a beautifully relaxed way to explore the city, and a great way to get your bearings before setting off to explore the sights in more detail.

Parkwijk

Parkwijk is close to the roads to Amsterdam and the A9. The center of Haarlem is a stone's throw away of about ten minutes by bike. The neighborhood is characterized by many flats, but also many recent new buildings. It is a spacious area with lots of greenery and a large park, the Burgemeester Reinaldapark. Around 6,850 people live in Parkwijk, including many over-65s. This is partly due to the Reinaldahuis, a care home in the neighborhood. Parkwijk can be subdivided into 3 smaller neighborhoods; De Architectenbuurt (from Amsterdamsevaart up to and including Bazellaan), De Kunstschildersbuurt (from Bazellaan to Mien Ruyspad). The neighborhood is represented in the Parkwijk / Zuiderpolder neighborhood council, which is located in Wijkcentrum "de Sprong" (the former school building of the Viersprong). Many houses from the original construction period were outdated and have since been replaced. Recent construction projects are: Haarlem School, Hof van Hendrik and the Groene Linten. As a result, in 2014 more than 20% of the neighborhood consisted of new homes. The neighborhood is dominated by mid-rise: 7 out of 10 houses are flats. 70% of the homes are social housing units; for the restructuring around 1990 this was still 90%.  

Services

Parkwijk is centrally located between the two residential areas and the small-scale neighborhood shopping center Zuiderpark. Where among other things a DekaMarkt, hairdresser, shop, butcher, Turkish shops, flower kiosk, pet shop, bicycle shop and much more can be found. In addition, Fit Factory houses a gym directly opposite the square.The district also benefits from the large city park, which gives the district a green character. In terms of education, Parkwijk has a branch of the Zuiderpolder OBS located in the Zuiderpolder.In addition to the District Center, a green oasis was created in May 2016, which is appropriately called "East Oasis".

Jiu Jutsu

Jiu Jitsu isn’t called ” Brazilian Jiu Jitsu” it is simply called ” Jiu Jitsu ” . When the world found out about the origins of Jiu Jitsu, because it was tied to the Gracie family, many people began calling it ” Gracie Jiu Jitsu ” or “Brazilian Jiu Jitsu ” . As a rule of thumb calling it Jiu Jitsu one can never go wrong describing it to the unaware. There are now two categories of Jiu Jitsu. Sport Jiu Jitsu , where you see tournaments and academies and students training for competition. The goal in Sport Jiu Jitsu is to win by submission or by points. The other side of Jiu Jitsu is called Self Defense Jiu Jitsu, where the focus is mainly self protection of encounters that might happen in the street. The early days of Jiu Jitsu were held in the highest regards because of it’s effectiveness in ending a fight quickly, you can see with the early days of the UFC how Royce Gracie used Jiu Jitsu to finish fights .
